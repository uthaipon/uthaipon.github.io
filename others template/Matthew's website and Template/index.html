About

I am a second-year PhD student at the Georgia Institute of Technology in the School of Computer Science. My research interests include analytic and enumerative combinatorics, graph mining, Markov chain Monte Carlo methods, and randomized algorithms. My advisor is Dana Randall, and I am generously supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. As an undergraduate, I studied computer science and mathematics at the University of Kentucky. For more details, see my CV.
